(NEXSTAR) \u2013\u00a0Does it feel like a totally different climate when you go across town? It may be the urban heat island effect. <\/p>\n

The way a city is designed can make hot weather feel even worse. The temperature on your block is influenced by everything from the number of trees on the street to the color of the pavement. When trees and vegetation (which absorb heat) are replaced by buildings and roads (which can radiate heat), it feels hotter. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe heat island effect can result in significant temperature differences between rural and urban areas,\u201d explains the Environmental Protection Agency<\/a>.<\/p>\n
